Sushi Bake

Description

This sushi bake recipe is a delicious and easy way to enjoy sushi flavors without the hassle of rolling. It's perfect for gatherings or a cozy night in.

Enjoy the flavors of sushi in a baked form, making it easier to serve and share!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds salmon fillet
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon lemon pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 cups sushi rice (rinsed)
  • 2 3/4 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 8 ounces imitation crab (shredded and chopped)
  • 8 ounces softened cream cheese
  • 1 cup kewpie mayonnaise, divided
  • 3 tablespoons Sriracha, plus more to taste, divided
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 3 large sheets nori(roasted seaweed)
  • 2 teaspoons everything bagel seasoning
  • 2 teaspoons lime juice
  • 2 sliced green onions, or more to taste
  • 1 avocado - peeled, pitted, and diced

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment or foil.
  2. Place salmon on the baking sheet; sprinkle with 1/2 teaspoon salt, pepper, lemon pepper, garlic powder, and paprika. Drizzle oil over salmon.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until salmon just starts to flake easily with a fork, 10 to 12 minutes.
  4. Let cool slightly before breaking up into small pieces. Reduce oven temperature to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  5. Meanwhile, combine rice, water, and remaining salt in a sauce pan. Bring to a boil, stir once, reduce heat to low, and cover.
  6. Cook until liquid is absorbed and rice is tender, 12 to 14 minutes. Transfer to a large bowl and fluff with a fork. Stir in rice vinegar and let cool.
  7. When salmon is cooled, add it to a bowl with crab, cream cheese, 1/2 cup mayonnaise, 1 1/2 tablespoons Sriracha, and soy sauce, and stir to combine.
  8. Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish; add rice and press into an even layer. Crush nori sheets over rice and sprinkle with furikake.
  9. Spread salmon mixture over seaweed; sprinkle evenly with everything seasoning.
  10. Bake at 400 degrees F (200 degrees C) until browned around the edges, about 25 minutes.
  11. Stir remaining 1/2 cup mayonnaise, remaining 1 1/2 tablespoons Sriracha, and lime juice together.
  12. Drizzle over casserole before serving; top with green onions and avocado.
